# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers on LLM Output Diversity & Monoculture

1. **Generative Monoculture in Large Language Models** (2407.02209_generative_monoculture.pdf)
   - Authors: Fan Wu, Emily Black, Varun Chandrasekaran
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2407.02209
   - Why relevant: Defines "generative monoculture" — LLMs narrowing output diversity relative to training data. Core concept for our research.

2. **We're Different, We're the Same: Creative Homogeneity Across LLMs** (2501.19361_creative_homogeneity_across_llms.pdf)
   - Authors: Emily Wenger, Yoed Kenett
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2501.19361
   - Why relevant: Shows homogeneity persists across different LLMs, not just within one model. Uses AUT, DAT, Forward Flow tests.

3. **Correlated Errors in Large Language Models** (2506.07962_correlated_errors_llms.pdf)
   - Authors: Various
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2506.07962
   - Why relevant: Shows LLMs are more correlated with each other than with ground truth; temperature/prompts don't fix monoculture.

## Creativity Evaluation Frameworks

4. **CreativityPrism: A Holistic Evaluation Framework for LLM Creativity** (2510.20091_creativity_prism.pdf)
   - Authors: Zhaoyi Joey Hou et al.
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2510.20091
   - Why relevant: Comprehensive framework measuring quality, novelty, and diversity. Shows novelty poorly correlates with other metrics.

5. **Beyond Divergent Creativity: A Human-Based Evaluation of Creativity in LLMs** (2601.20546_beyond_divergent_creativity.pdf)
   - Authors: Various
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2601.20546
   - Why relevant: Introduces CDAT measuring novelty conditional on appropriateness. Shows alignment shifts models toward appropriate but less creative.

6. **Divergent Creativity in Humans and Large Language Models** (2405.13012_divergent_creativity_humans_llms.pdf)
   - Authors: Various
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2405.13012
   - Why relevant: Compares human and LLM divergent thinking using DAT with 100K human baseline.
